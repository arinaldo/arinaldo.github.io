I am a Professor in the Department of Department of Statistics and Data Science at The University of Texas at Austin.
I am broadly interested in the theoretical properties of high-dimensional statistical models. I have worked in high-dimensional regression, time series, privacy, categorical data analysis and graphical modeling, statistical network analysis, algebraic statistics, density-based clustering and non-parametric density estimation, topological and geometric data analysis, change-point detection, ranking, and reinforcement learning.

Before joining UT Austin, I was a Professor in the Department of Statistics and Data Science at Carnegie Mellon University, where I obtained my Ph.D. in Statistics in 2005 under the supervision of Stephen Fienberg.
