I study the theoretical properties of high-dimensional statistical models. I have worked in high-dimensional regression, time series, privacy, categorical data analysis, and graphical modeling, statistical network analysis, algebraic statistics, density-based clustering, non-parametric density estimation, topological and geometric data analysis, change-point detection, ranking, and reinforcement learning.

Before joining UT Austin, I was a Dean's Chair Professor in the Department of Statistics and Data Science and the Associate Dean of Research in the Dietrich College of Humanities and Social Sciences at Carnegie Mellon University, where I obtained my Ph.D. in Statistics in 2005 under the supervision of Stephen Fienberg.
